Spokane's Sustainability Action Plan (SAP) Scorecard

About this site

Welcome to Spokane's Sustainability Action Plan (SAP) Scorecard. This site helps inform the people of Spokane on the city's progress (or lack of) toward the plan's goals. The plan and its goals were set in 2021 with most baseline measurements set on values from 2016.

Click to expand for more info.

You can read the SAP on the city website here.

The plan was a recognition of the threat climate change poses to our region. The primary goal of the plan is to reduce green house gas emissions. Per the plan:

Reduce communitywide greenhouse gas emissions from 2016 baseline by:

45% by 2030 to 1,159,838 metric tons
70% by 2040 to 632,639 metric tons
95% by 2050 to 105,440 metric tons and achieve net zero emissions

In addition to that overall goal, the plan also outlines 7 sector-specific goals. The cards below indicate the current status towards those goals.

Glossary

Baseline: The level of emissions for the city when current goals were set. 2016 in most cases.

GHG: Greenhouse gas.

KPI: Key Performance Indicator; a measure of how the city is doing.

MT: Mega tons; one million metric tons. Typically, C02 (carbon dioxide) or CO2 equivalents.
