About this site

Welcome to Spokane Scorecard. This site began by focusing on the city's Sustainabily Action Plan (SAP), last published in 2021. This site helps inform the people of Spokane on the city's progress toward the plan's goals. Most baseline measurements set on values from 2016. This site is produced by a resident of Spokane, it is not affiliated with the city. So if you find an error or have a question, please send them here.


More details

You can see the SAP on the city website here.

The plan was a recognition of the threat climate change poses to our region. The primary goal of the plan is to reduce green house gas emissions. Per the plan:

Reduce communitywide greenhouse gas emissions from 2016 baseline by:

45% by 2030 to 1,159,838 metric tons
70% by 2040 to 632,639 metric tons
95% by 2050 to 105,440 metric tons and achieve net zero emissions

In addition to that overall goal, the plan also outlines 7 sector-specific goals. The cards below indicate the current status towards those goals.

Since the publication of Spokane's SAP, the state legislature passed HB 1181 which set forth new requirements for regional comprehensive plans to include an element on climate change and resiliency. As a result, this site intends to be a more broad scorecard for the city, as its climate goals are likely to expand into others areas of city planning vs simply the accounting of GHG emissions.

Glossary

Baseline: The level of emissions for the city when current goals were set. 2016 in most cases.

GHG: Greenhouse gas.

KPI: Key Performance Indicator; a measure of how the city is doing.

MT: Mega tons; one million metric tons. Typically, C02 (carbon dioxide) or CO2 equivalents.
